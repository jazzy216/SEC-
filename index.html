The CIA Triad

The CIA triad is a fundamental model in information security designed to guide policies for information security within an organization.

Confidentiality

Preventing unauthorized disclosure of information.

  • Encryption (Data at rest/transit)
  • Access Controls (Need to know)
  • Steganography

Integrity

Assuring data has not been intentionally or accidentally modified.

  • Hashing (SHA-256, MD5)
  • Digital Signatures
  • Certificates

Availability

Ensuring timely and reliable access to information.

  • Redundancy (RAID, Clusters)
  • Backups
  • DDoS Mitigation

Exam Tip: When a question asks about preventing unauthorized changes to data, the answer almost always involves Integrity (Hashing). If it asks about preventing unauthorized viewing, it's Confidentiality (Encryption).
